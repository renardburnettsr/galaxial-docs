Acceptable use policy

Last updated: June 2026

Use Galaxial for good

We built Galaxial to help people think, work, and remember better. We want it to stay a safe and useful place for everyone.

Things that are fine

  • Personal projects, business work, research, learning
  • Uploading your own documents and notes
  • Using Galaxial to organise, recall, and build on your own ideas

Things that are not allowed

  • Using Galaxial to harass, threaten, or harm anyone
  • Uploading illegal content or content that violates someone else's rights
  • Trying to use Galaxial to generate harmful, misleading, or deceptive content at scale
  • Attempting to break into the platform or abuse the API
  • Sharing your account or using it on behalf of someone else without telling us

What happens if you break the rules

We may suspend or permanently ban accounts that violate this policy. In serious cases, we may report activity to the appropriate authorities.

Questions

If you're unsure whether something is allowed, just ask: hello@galaxial.app

2. Permitted use

The Service may be used for lawful personal, educational, and commercial purposes consistent with these terms and all applicable laws and regulations.

3. Prohibited activities

Users may not use the Service to:

  • Violate any applicable local, national, or international law or regulation

  • Conduct automated scraping, crawling, or data extraction without written permission
  • Attempt to gain unauthorised access to any systems, networks, or data
  • Generate or distribute misinformation, spam, or deceptive content at scale

4. Enforcement

The Company reserves the right to investigate suspected violations and to suspend or terminate access to the Service, with or without notice, for any violation of this AUP. The Company may report unlawful activities to law enforcement authorities.

5. Reporting violations

To report a suspected violation of this AUP, contact hello@galaxial.app.

6. Modifications

The Company may update this AUP at any time. Continued use of the Service following notification of changes constitutes acceptance of the revised AUP.
